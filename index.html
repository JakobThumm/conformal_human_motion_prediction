Our certifiable pipeline running on a real Franka Emika robot: it estimates the 3D human pose and its uncertainty from stereo vision, predicts future motion with conformal prediction sets, and stops the robot before a collision can occur — even for out-of-distribution inputs.

Abstract

Safe human-robot collaboration (HRC) requires accurate human pose estimation and motion prediction to prevent critical collisions. Existing certifiable safe HRC approaches are highly conservative or rely on marker-based motion tracking, while vision-based pose estimators lack the statistical guarantees required for certification in accordance with ISO 13849-1.

Hence, we propose a pipeline that predicts 3D human motion with end-to-end propagation of the pose estimation uncertainty and over-approximates the prediction error with conformal prediction sets. A gradient-based monitor detects out-of-distribution inputs and replaces them with past predictions to maintain smooth operation. The resulting conformal prediction sets directly integrate into the provably safe HRC approach SARA shield.

In experiments on the Human3.6M dataset and a real-world HRC setting, our conformal prediction sets reduce the conservatism of model-based predictions by a factor of 8, and our framework achieves a probability of a dangerous failure per hour below 10−6 with 99.99 % confidence — sufficient for certification with performance level d.

How It Works

Methodological overview. Our novel contributions are highlighted in blue. From the past 2 s of stereo images we estimate an uncertainty-aware 3D pose, predict future poses with heteroscedastic covariances, and convert them into conformal prediction sets. Pose and motion monitors detect out-of-distribution inputs. The conformal sets bound the human reachable occupancy, which SARA shield uses to certify the safety of the robot trajectory.

1 Uncertainty-aware 3D pose

A YOLO26 pose network returns per-keypoint 2D means and covariances in both camera frames. Uncertainty-aware triangulation lifts them to a 3D pose with full covariance matrices, giving a calibrated estimate of where each human joint is.

2 Uncertainty-aware motion prediction

A DCT-based pose transformer predicts the next 3D poses together with heteroscedastic aleatoric covariances. The input pose uncertainty is embedded and propagated end-to-end, so the predicted covariances reflect both estimation and prediction error.

3 Conformal prediction sets

Using split conformal prediction, we calibrate spherical prediction sets that contain the true future joint positions with 1 − ε confidence. Spheres and capsules enable fast intersection checks while retaining a finite-sample coverage guarantee.

4 Out-of-distribution handling

A sketched-Lanczos uncertainty monitor flags inputs that fall outside the training distribution. Rather than emitting an unreliable prediction, the pipeline reuses past motion predictions, degrading gracefully and resuming once valid observations return.

5 Certifiable safety via SARA shield

The conformal prediction sets bound the human reachable occupancy over the robot's braking horizon. SARA shield verifies each monitored trajectory against this occupancy and executes it only if a collision can be excluded — providing certifiable safety at all times.

Results

We evaluate on the Human3.6M benchmark and a real-world Franka Emika HRC setup, testing three hypotheses: our conformal sets are less conservative than the ISO 13855 velocity model at equal coverage (H1); the full system reaches PFHD < 10−6, i.e. PL d (H2); and OOD handling reduces invalid predictions at a small accuracy cost (H3).

Real-World Deployment

We integrated the pipeline into SARA shield and deployed it in a real-world HRC setting on a Franka Emika robot, using an Intel RealSense 435i camera for perception and depth. We mark all 3D poses whose depth differs by more than 0.8 m from the median depth as out-of-distribution, so the OOD monitor catches perception failures directly. In every tested instance, the robot came to a complete stop before the human operator could reach it. Our OOD handling reduces interruptions by 23.8 % compared to naively rejecting every uncertain input, at a cost of only 1.9 % higher MPJPE (H3).
